- My exam experience

## What is the OSCP and why it was important?
The Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP) is a well-known certification in the industry and treated as the standard level of credentials for a qualified penetration tester. Now, I would argue that there are other courses out there that have better material and labs than the OSCP, however, the exam being proctored and restricted to a time limit of 24 hours gives the certification its credibility. For a lot of organisations, this qualification seems to be used as the baseline for a role in offensive security.

## What was the content?
The OSCP teaches you the basics such as port scanning, fuzzing, and simple web attacks. It also goes over some simple windows/linux privilege escalation techniques and Active Directory attacks.

## How did I study for it?
I wanted to complete the OSCP in 3 months. Since I was doing the course while working, I found that being consistent about study was super important. Planning the study into my schedule made it much easier to stay discipled and make progress steadily.
